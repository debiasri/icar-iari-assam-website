About ICAR-IARI Assam

History & Background

The ICAR-Indian Agricultural Research Institute (IARI), Assam is anchored at Dirpai Chapori, Gogamukh, Dhemaji, Assam along the Subansiri riverbank. The institute is spread over 587 hectares of land — providing ample space for research, extension and teaching.

The academic journey started with the foundation stone laying on May 26, 2017 by the Prime Minister, Shri Narendra Modi. The formal inauguration was done by Union Minister Shri Arjun Munda on 4th March 2024.

The institute aims to undertake systematic research for developing efficient agricultural technologies, contributing to the 'Second Green Revolution' in Northeast India. Education, research and outreach are the core objectives.

Mission & Vision
🎯 Mission
To serve as a premier institution for higher agricultural education, research and extension in Northeast India, developing technologies for food security, sustainability and farmers' welfare.
🔭 Vision
To become a hub of higher agricultural education and research in South-East Asia, with special focus on the unique agro-ecological systems of the Northeast.
Mandates & Objectives
  • ✓ To serve as an institution of learning in higher agricultural education in South-East Asia
  • ✓ To conserve and utilize high-value biotic resources of Northeast India
  • ✓ To improve crop and plant genotypes having speciality traits
  • ✓ To manage acid soils and develop technologies for productivity maximization
  • ✓ To develop organic farming modules for major cropping systems
  • ✓ To promote agri-education and realize the Second Green Revolution in Northeast India

Schools / Divisions

🌿 Division of Crop Improvement

Focused on development of high-yielding, stress-tolerant varieties of rice, pulses, oilseeds and other crops adapted to Northeast agro-ecology. Key activities include:

  • » Rice breeding for flood and drought tolerance
  • » Joha rice aroma QTL mapping
  • » Black rice varietal improvement
  • » Pulse crop improvement for acid soils
  • » Mustard hybrid development
🍊 Division of Horticulture

Research and development in fruits, vegetables, flowers, spices and medicinal plants unique to Northeast India. Key activities include:

  • » Flower and ornamental horticulture research
  • » Vegetable variety trials
  • » Medicinal & aromatic plant research
  • » Post-harvest technology development
  • » Tea and large cardamom research
🐄 Division of Animal Science & Fisheries

Livestock, poultry, fisheries and integrated farming systems research for comprehensive agricultural development in the region:

  • » Ornamental fish culture & aquaculture
  • » Integrated farming systems (IFS)
  • » Poultry breed development for NE
  • » Livestock improvement programmes
  • » Skill development in fish processing

Ph.D. and M.Sc. admissions are through the ICAR All India Entrance Examination for Admission (ICAR-AIEEA), conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA).

  • » Appear for the entrance examination
  • » Merit-based counselling and allotment
